# The Growth Engine

For decades, finance departments have categorized translation as a necessary expense—a "tax" on doing business internationally. In the modern digital economy, this view is obsolete.

Localization is a growth lever. When executed with precision, it lowers customer acquisition costs (CAC) and increases lifetime value (LTV). The math is simple: People do not buy what they cannot understand.

## The "Can't Read, Won't Buy" Reality

The Common Sense Advisory (CSA Research) conducted a landmark study of 8,709 consumers across 29 countries. The findings were definitive:

*   **76%** of online shoppers prefer to buy products with information in their native language.
*   **40%** will never buy from websites in other languages.

This means that failing to localize doesn't just reduce conversion—it effectively caps your total addressable market (TAM) at roughly half its potential.

## Hard Metrics: Conversion & Retention

Beyond mere preference, localization impacts the bottom line metrics that growth teams track daily.

### 1. The Conversion Lift
When payment platform Stripe enabled localized payment methods and checkout flows in distinct European markets, they observed a significant uptick. Offering a culturally relevant experience led to a **7.4% increase in checkout conversion**. In e-commerce, a 7% lift can effectively double the profit margin.

### 2. The Loyalty Factor (Retention)
Acquiring a customer is expensive; keeping them is cheap. Research indicates that companies with high-quality localization strategies see **25% higher customer retention rates** in international markets.

> "Localization goes beyond translation—it creates experiences that feel native to each market, which makes customers feel understood."
