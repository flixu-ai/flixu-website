# Flixu vs. Lokalise: The Missing Middle

Lokalise has built a fantastic product for a very specific problem: extracting UI strings from source code. If you are a developer tasked with mapping JSON keys in a complex mobile application, Lokalise offers an incredibly mature, feature-rich ecosystem.

But what if you aren't just translating UI strings? What if you are translating marketing copy, support documentation, product catalogs, and long-form content? And what if you want to achieve this using cutting-edge AI, without paying legacy software taxes?

This is where the "Missing Middle" comes in, and why teams are migrating from UI-centric tools to AI-native Context Orchestrators like Flixu.

## 1. The Pricing Philosophy: Seats vs. Output

The most significant divergence between Flixu and traditional localization platforms like Lokalise is the business model.

Lokalise utilizes a two-dimensional pricing matrix: **Seats + Hosted Words**. 
If you want to invite your Product Manager just to review a translation in Spanish, you have to pay for an additional seat. If you have a large product catalog sitting translation memory, you pay for hosting those words even if you never touch them.

**Flixu believes in Output-Only Billing.**
Localization is a team sport. Your developers, marketers, and country managers should naturally collaborate in the same workspace. Flixu's Team and Business plans include multi-seat access, and you are billed exclusively for the value created: the characters generated by the AI engine. 

## 2. String-Based Translation vs. Context Orchestration

Lokalise was born in the era of string-by-string human translation. The tool's UI is fundamentally a grid of isolated sentences. When AI is bolted onto this paradigm, it operates as a "pass-through" endpoint—blindly translating one string at a time.

Flixu was engineered from the ground up for the AI era. It utilizes a **5-Dimensional Context Engine**. When you request a translation, Flixu doesn't just look at the sentence. It looks at:

- The overall **Document Context** and logical flow.
- The established **Brand Voice** (e.g., formal B2B vs. playful B2C).
- Your unified **Glossary** of protected terms.
- The historical **Translation Memory**, retrieved via high-dimensional vector search.

The AI knows *who* is speaking, *who* is reading, and *how* your brand should sound.

## 3. The True Cost of Setup

Integrating a string-management platform can be a multi-week engineering project. Because it is designed to hook directly into your repository, the initial friction is high.

Flixu provides a simpler path for agile teams. With our API-first orchestration, you can be up and running in minutes. Import your existing TMX (Translation Memory) and CSV (Glossary) files, and the AI instantly adopts your historical linguistic identity.

## Honest Recommendation

**Choose Lokalise if:**
You have a large, complex software repository, a dedicated localization engineering team, and your primary goal is managing the CI/CD pipeline of nested UI string keys.

**Choose Flixu if:**
You want the highest quality AI translations for software, marketing, and documents, you want to eliminate the "seat tax" so your whole team can collaborate, and you want a native context engine that strictly adheres to your Brand Voice right out of the box.
